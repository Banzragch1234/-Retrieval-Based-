2. Tamacha, the son of Batsagaan, Khorichar the wise, Khorichar the wise's
son Uujim Buural, Uujim Buural's son Sal-khachau, Sal-khachau's son
Ikhnuden, Ikhnuden's son Shinsochi, Shinsochi's son Kharchu.
3. Borjigidai, the wise man, is the son of Kharchu. Borjigidai, the wise man's wife is
Mongoljingua, and his son is Torgoljin Bayan. Torgoljin Bayan, the wise man, has a
wife named Borogchingua, a young servant named Boroldaisuyalbi, and two horses.
Torgoljin Bayan's sons are Duva Sohor and Dobu Wise Man. 4. Duva Sohor, the wise
man, has one eye in his forehead, and three nomadic people.
5. One day, Lord Duva went up to the mountain with his blind brother and Dobu his wise
brother. Duva is blind, if God looks from above, a group of citizens will move to Tunkheleg
stream.
6. Duwa the blind man said, "In that nomadic village, in front of the cart stall, there is a
beautiful girl sitting. If you have not yet found a wife, wise brother Dobu will ask you to
marry her." So he sent his wise brother Dobu to show her to him.
7. The wise man, when he reached the village, saw the girl, and he found that she was a very famous and
beautiful girl, and had never been married to anyone. Her name was Alun Gua.
8. This girl was born to Bargujin Gua, the wife of Khorilardai the Wise, the lord of the Hori Tumd,
in a place called Arig Us, a land of the Hori Tumd (Khori Buryats). Her mother Bargujin Gua was
the daughter of Bargudai the Wise, the lord of the Bargujin Tokhum in a distant land. That group
of people belonged to Khorilardai the Wise.
9. The wise man Horilardai, who had forbidden the hunting of deer such as sable and squirrel in
the lands of the Hori Tumans, quarreled with each other and separated. He heard that the Horilar
tribe had abundant deer in the Burhan Khaldun Mountains and moved to meet the Shinch Bayan
Uriankhai, the lord of Burhan Khaldun. This is how the ruler of the Hori Tumans asked for Alun-
gua, the daughter of the wise man Horilardai, who was born in the Ari river, and made her the wife
of the wise man Dobu.
10. Alun-gua came to the wise man Dobu and gave birth to two sons named
Beludu and Belgunu.
11. Duwa, the blind brother, had four sons. After Duwa died, his four sons, not
considering Dobu the wise man as their uncle, separated and moved away,
becoming four clans.
12. One day, when Dobu the Wise Togotsog went out to hunt on a high mountain,
he met a man in the forest who was killing a deer and roasting its ribs.
13. When the wise man said, "My friend, take the spear," the man took the spear
and the skin himself and gave the rest to the wise man.
14. As the wise man Dobu was carrying deer meat, he met a poor man on the road who was carrying
his child.
15. "What kind of person are you?" asked the wise man Dobu. The man replied, "I
am from Maligba. I am tired now. Give me some of the meat of that deer. I will
give you my son."
16. The wise man Dobu agreed, gave the man the leg of the deer, and brought the
boy home to serve him.
17. Meanwhile, the wise man Dobu passed away. After the wise man Dobu passed away
His wife, Alun-gua, became pregnant and gave birth to three sons,
Bukha Khatagi, Bukhatu Salji, and Bodonchar Munkhag.
18. The two sons Belgunu and Begudu, who were born during the lifetime of the wise
man Dobu, discussed this with their mother Alun-gua: "Our mother, without a male
relative or a male relative, gave birth to these three sons. There is only one servant of
the Malig Begud clan in the house. These three sons are his children," Alun-gua learned
of their discussion with their mother.
19. One spring day, Belgunutei, Beseduutei, Bukha Khatagi, Bukhatu Salji, and Bodonchar
cooked dried mutton and fed it to their five foolish sons. They sat them down side by side,
gave each one a stick (or thin stick) to break, and when they were told to break it, they easily
broke it. Also, when they tied five sticks together and told them to break them, they could not
break all five of them in a row.
20. At this, Alun-gua's mother said: "You and Belgunu and Pedebu are
suspicious about how I gave birth to these three sons, and whose sons they
are. Your suspicions are correct.
21. But you do not know why. Every night, a white-yellow man, shining with a household
lamp, comes and touches my belly, and his light penetrates my belly. That man, like the
rising sun, appears yellow like a yellow dog on the border of the moon. What do you say
in vain? From that, they are the sons of heaven. Why do you compare them to a black
man? When the king becomes the most powerful, the black man will know why."
22. And Alun-gua, having instructed his five sons, said: "You five sons, are not born
from my one womb. If you separate yourselves, you will be as sharp as arrows, and
easily defeated by men. If you unite, you will be as sharp as five arrows, and easily
defeated by no one." At that moment, Alun-gua's mother passed away.
23. After the death of mother Alun-gua, the five brothers decided to divide their property and livestock. Belgunu, Peydugu,
Bukha Khatagi, and Bukhatu Salji divided their property among the four of them, and Bodonchar, the younger brother, was
considered a fool and did not give him a share, disregarding his inheritance.
24. Bodonchar is not counted in the womb, so what are you doing here? The tailed orog Shinkhul
rode his horse, thinking, "If I die, I will die, if I live, I will live," and set off towards the Onon River.
There, he arrived at a place called Baljian Island and built a grass hut.
25. Meanwhile, he saw a black sparrow catching and eating a brown bear, so he
set a trap with a pair of horsehair snakes and caught the brown bear.
26. Bodonchar, because he had nothing to eat, would hunt down and kill the deer that the wolves had caught, or
he would pick up the carcasses that the wolves had eaten, feeding his stomach and feeding his eyes. That year
passed.

31. They told the villagers: "Every day a man comes to the mountain to drink eseg (tsegee). That

32. Soon a man came running up the Tunkheleg River. When he arrived, it was Bodonchar. His
brother, Bukha Khatagi, recognized him and rode with him up the Onon River.
33. Bodonchar, Bukha Khatagi, followed behind the elder brother and said, "Brother, brother, the one with

34. When Bodonchar said the same thing a second time, his brother did not answer. When

35. Bodonchar said: "The citizens of Tugaar (the million) who have no master, big
and small, good and bad, and bald are all the same. Since they are such easy
citizens, let us conquer them."
36. His brother said: "Well then, let's go home and consult with our brothers, and let

37. When he arrived home, he consulted with the brothers and set out. The
leaders also sent Bodonchar.
38. Bodonchar led the way and caught a pregnant woman and asked her, "What kind of person
are you?" The woman replied, "I am a man of the Adankhan Uriankhai tribe." 39. From there,
the five brothers attacked the people, took their horses and cattle, and made them slaves.
40. That pregnant woman came to Bodonchar and gave birth to a son. He was called
Jajiradai, because he was a son of a foreign tribe. He became the ancestor of Jadaran.
That son of Jajiradai was called Tugudai. Tugudai's son was Buri-bulchiru, Buri-
bulchiru's son was Khar-khadaan. Khar-khadaan's son was Jamuha. They became the
Jadaran tribe. (Jada means foreign tribe).
41. That woman also gave birth to a son from Bodonchar. He was named Baardai, because he was the son
of the woman who had taken him. He became the ancestor of Baar. Baardai's son was Zutgalboh, and
Zutgalboh took many wives, so his sons were as numerous as Manan. They became the descendants of
Manan Baar.

57. After Ambagai Khan was captured and Khadaan and Hotala were named, all the Mongols
and Thais gathered at a place called the Khorkhuna Valley on the Onon River, discussed it, and

the king and shaved the ribs and knees under the Khorkhuna tree.
They danced until the ground was bare and they got married.
